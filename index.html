ONIGIRI

Onigiri is a Japanese rice ball made from steamed rice, typically shaped into a triangle or cylinder and often wrapped in nori (seaweed). It is a popular, portable food that can be eaten as a snack or meal and is usually filled with ingredients like salted salmon, pickled plum (umeboshi).

Home

Instructions 1. Cook the Rice Wash the rice in a colander under cold water until the water runs clear. Cook the rice in a saucepan or a rice cooker following the manufacturer's instructions. Once cooked, let the rice steam for about 10-15 minutes, then transfer it to a bowl and allow it to cool until it's warm enough to handle comfortably. 2. Prepare Your Hands (or Mold) To prevent the rice from sticking, wet your hands with water and rub a pinch of salt between your palms. If using an onigiri mold, wet the inside of the mold instead. 3. Shape and Fill the Onigiri Take a portion of the warm, cooked rice in your hands (or fill the mold halfway). • For filled onigiri: Create an indentation in the center of the rice with your finger, add about a teaspoon of your desired filling, and then cover the filling with a bit more rice. Gently press the rice together. • For seasoned onigiri: Mix the cooked rice with furikake seasoning before shaping. 4. Form the Shape Gently but firmly press the rice into a ball, disc, or the traditional triangle shape, rotating it in your hands a few times. The pressure should be firm enough to compact the rice so it holds its shape, but not so firm that it becomes dense or mushy. 5. Wrap with Nori Wrap the bottom of the onigiri with a strip or a small square of nori seaweed. This helps with holding the rice ball and adds flavor. Onigiri is best enjoyed immediately after preparation. If you are not serving them right away, wrap them individually in plastic wrap to keep the rice from drying out.
